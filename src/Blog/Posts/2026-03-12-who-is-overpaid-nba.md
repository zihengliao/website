## Who Is Overpaid in the NBA?

I was scrolling r/nba on Reddit the other day as one usually does and I saw an interesting post that caught my eye. 

![Reddit post on Mikal Bridges](/assets/blog/2026-03-12-who-is-overpaid-nba/reddit_post.png)

A couple of questions came to mind upon seeing this.
- How much is a first round pick worth?
- How do you determine the dollar amount a player is worth?
- Who is the most overpaid athlete in the NBA?

Today we will try to answer the last question. Who is the most overpaid athlete in the NBA?

There are a couple ways to interpret and answer this question. Do we approach this in relative terms where a player gets paid $2 million but only sees 1 or 2 minutes of playing time a game? Or absolute terms where a player may be paid a max contract but not performing to the level expected for the size of that salary.

We will be going with the latter where we look at salary vs on the court impact.

There is probably a hundred different statistics to measure impact, but they all have their own flaws. For example, (+/-) measures how many points the team scores when the player is on the court. But this doesn't account for teammates or even opponents.

The box plus minus (BPM) measures individual performance based on recorded stats on the box score. However the flaw of this is that it doesn't take into account for actions that aren't recorded such as spacing, screening and overall just good basketball IQ plays. These are players that pass the "eye test" but aren't reflected on the stat sheet such as Alex Caruso or Draymond Green.

The point I'm trying to make is that without context, no individual stat is capable of encapsulating the value of a player. 

In saying that, I will be using the player's net rating as an initial way to find players with poor performance relative to their salary. 

A player's net rating can be defined as the team's offensive rating $-$ the team's defensive rating when the player is on the court. 

What I'm trying to measure here is how much impact a player has on the team and using this to judge their value.

![Net Rating vs Salary Correlation](/assets/blog/2026-03-12-who-is-overpaid-nba/rating_salary_corr.png)

This is a plot of every single active NBA player in the 2025-26 season of their salary compared to their net rating.

As we can see, generally players with a strong impact towards the game tend to get paid more.

![Net Rating vs Salary Correlation](/assets/blog/2026-03-12-who-is-overpaid-nba/positive_net_rating_players.png)

We have big name franchise players such as Jokic, Curry and Giannis getting paid the big bucks whilst being solid contributers to their teams.

![Net Rating vs Salary Correlation](/assets/blog/2026-03-12-who-is-overpaid-nba/zach_lavine_salary_net_rating.png)

On the other end of the spectrum we have Zach LaVine getting paid $47,499,660 in the 2025-26 season which makes him a top 10 highest paid player this season over players like SGA and Luka Doncic. Despite this, he is having one of the worst net ratings in the league at -12.6.

Net rating doesn't tell the full story or can be misleading sometimes, so it's necessary to investigate a bit deeper.

![Net Rating vs Salary Correlation](/assets/blog/2026-03-12-who-is-overpaid-nba/zach_lavine_raw_stats.png)

Despite having relatively good efficiency, Zach LaVine's stats aren't anything extraordinary that gives justice to his salary this season.

If we compare some of his stats relative to the rest of the league, he is ranked:
- 52nd in PPG
- 40th in eFG%
- 45th in 3P%
- 191st in Assists

yet the 10th highest paid NBA player this season.

He is not a high volume scorer, nor a play maker, he also isn't a great defender.

By no means is Zach LaVine a bad player statistically, but he definitely isn't playing well enough to be getting paid $47,499,660 this season. The "eye test" backs it up as well.

### Concluding Statement

Zach LaVine is by no means a bad player. He is former 6th man of the year, 2x all star and 2x dunk contest champion. However, his impact on the court seems to be diminishing each year since his Chicago days.

LaVine's contract doesn't end until the end of next season, but it is unlikely he will see a contract of this size again.

The question now becomes, how much is he worth and how much should he be paid?
